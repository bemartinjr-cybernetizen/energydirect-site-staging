Switching in Victoria is simpler than it looks

There is no need to call AEP Texas or schedule a technician. Switching is mostly a paperwork check on the plan you are leaving and the plan you are joining. Here is the order that keeps it smooth.

Step 1: Review your current contract

  • End date: note when your current term expires.
  • Early-termination fee: check whether leaving early costs anything.
  • What you liked or didn't: read your current EFL so you switch toward better terms, not just a different logo.

Step 2: Compare new plans by ZIP

  • Enter your ZIP to see the plans actually offered at your Victoria address.
  • Open each EFL and line up contract length, energy charges, delivery charges, and fees.
  • Match the plan to your usage, since the best plan for an apartment differs from the best plan for a house.

Step 3: Watch the timeline

Switching timing depends on the plan and on AEP Texas processing. You will see the expected start date during enrollment and on your confirmation email. AEP Texas keeps delivering power the whole time, so there is no gap when you switch.

Step 4: Submit and save your documents

Have your service address and identification ready, and confirm any deposit requirement if prompted. After you switch, save the EFL, Terms of Service, and confirmation, and check the final bill from your previous provider.

Victoria switching questions

Will my power be interrupted when I switch?

No. AEP Texas keeps delivering electricity without interruption while your new plan begins billing. Follow the enrollment steps to keep it seamless.

Who cancels my old plan?

The switch is usually coordinated through enrollment. Review your confirmation, and contact your previous provider only if your old contract needs a separate cancellation.

Do I need to pay a deposit?

Deposit requirements vary by plan and are shown before you submit. No-deposit options are available on qualifying plans.

How long does switching take?

Timing depends on the plan and on AEP Texas processing. Your expected start date is shown during checkout and on your confirmation.
