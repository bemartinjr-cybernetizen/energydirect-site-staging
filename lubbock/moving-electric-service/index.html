Lubbock now has retail electricity choice

Lubbock Power & Light has moved most of the city into the competitive ERCOT market, which means many Lubbock homes can now choose their own retail electric provider. A move is the natural moment to set up the plan you actually want.

What "choice" means for your move

In a competitive market, the company that delivers power to your meter is separate from the company you buy your plan from. The local utility keeps the poles, wires, and meters and restores outages. You pick the retail plan that sets your price, term, and billing.

  • Same wires, your choice of plan: switching providers never changes who keeps your lights on.
  • Confirm by ZIP: availability is rolling out by area, so a ZIP lookup is the reliable way to see what is offered at your new address.

Three move scenarios

  • Start (move-in): brand-new service at a new Lubbock address.
  • Transfer: carry your current plan to your new address when the plan allows it.
  • Stop: end service at the address you are leaving so you are not billed after move-out.

If you are switching from another provider as you move, check your old contract's end date and any early-termination fee before you set your start date.

How to set up power at your new Lubbock home

The whole process runs online and usually takes only a few minutes once you have your details ready.

Have these ready at enrollment

  • Service address for your new Lubbock home.
  • Move-in date you want power turned on.
  • Identification details the enrollment asks for.
  • Plan documents open to read: the EFL, Terms of Service, and Your Rights as a Customer.

Any deposit requirement is shown before you submit. No-deposit options are available on qualifying plans.

Timing and a smooth start

Requests are usually processed within about one business day, and there should be no interruption when the transfer is scheduled correctly. Enroll a few days before your move so your start date lands on move-in day and your confirmation shows exactly when service begins.

What to compare before you pick a plan

The Electricity Facts Label is the one document that tells you the truth about a plan. Open it for each plan you are weighing.

The numbers that move your bill

  • Price at 500 / 1,000 / 2,000 kWh: matched to your home size.
  • Minimum-usage fees: charges that hit if you use too little.
  • Bill credits: the usage level where any credit kicks in.
  • Delivery charges: the utility line items, listed separately.

The terms that move your options later

  • Rate type: fixed plans hold the energy charge for the term, variable plans can change month to month.
  • Contract length: and what happens at the end of the term.
  • Early-termination fee: what leaving early would cost.
  • Renewable content: the green percentage, if that matters to you.
