Setting up service for your move

Oncor delivers the power and handles the grid in Dallas. Your retail plan just sets your price, term, and billing.

Setting up service for your move

  • Enter your new ZIP: see the Ambit plans available at your new Dallas address.
  • Pick your plan: choose the plan that fits your new home.
  • Choose your start date: set service to begin the day you move in.
  • Ambit coordinates with Oncor: the start is handled for you, no separate call to the utility.
  • Lights on for move-in: your power is ready when you arrive.

Oncor still owns the poles, wires, and meters and restores outages no matter which plan you pick. Your retail plan only sets your price, term, and billing.

What to have ready

  • New address: the service address for your new Dallas home.
  • Move-in date: the day you want power turned on.
  • Identification: the ID details the enrollment asks for.
  • Plan documents: the Electricity Facts Label, Terms of Service, and Your Rights as a Customer, open to read.

Any deposit requirement is shown before you submit, so there are no surprises after you order.

Dallas move-in questions

How far ahead should I set up service before I move?

Enroll a few days ahead of your move so your start date is locked in. Same-day and next-day starts are common, and your confirmation shows the expected start date.

Will my power be on for move-in day?

Yes, when you enroll ahead and set your start date for move-in. The start is coordinated through enrollment so the lights are on when you arrive.

Who is the utility (TDU) in Dallas?

Oncor is the Transmission and Distribution Utility for most of the Dallas area. Oncor manages the lines, outages, and delivery regardless of your retail plan.

Is there any cost to compare or to get help?

No. Comparing plans and getting help from Energy Direct is free. You enroll directly in the plan you choose.
