Availability in Midland and the Oncor area

Midland sits in a deregulated part of the Oncor delivery area, which means you choose your retail provider. Comparing by ZIP is how you find the plan priced for your address.

Oncor delivers the power either way

Oncor is the Transmission and Distribution Utility for Midland and nearby Odessa. Oncor owns the poles, wires, and meters and restores outages no matter which plan you pick. Your retail plan only sets your price, term, and billing.

  • Same delivery, regulated charges: Oncor delivery charges apply to every provider equally.
  • Your menu varies: available terms and offers can differ across Midland ZIP codes.

Your home's usage changes the best plan

A small apartment and a large West Texas house rarely land on the same plan. The price that looks low at 1,000 kWh can shift once minimum-usage fees and bill credits apply at your real usage.

  • Match the plan to your usage, not to a headline rate.
  • Check the kWh tiers the plan prices at, so the number you see is the number you live with.

What to read on every Electricity Facts Label

The EFL is the one document that tells you the truth about a Midland plan. Open it for each plan before you enroll.

The numbers that move your bill

  • Price at 500 / 1,000 / 2,000 kWh: matched to your home size.
  • Minimum-usage fees: charges that hit if you use too little.
  • Bill credits: the usage level where any credit kicks in.
  • TDU delivery charges: the Oncor line items, listed separately.

The terms that move your options later

  • Rate type: fixed plans hold the energy charge for the term, variable plans can change month to month.
  • Contract length: and what happens at the end of the term.
  • Early-termination fee: what leaving early would cost.
  • Renewable content: the green percentage, if that matters to you.
