How choosing a Houston electricity plan actually works

Who does what in Houston

In the Houston area, CenterPoint Energy owns the poles, wires, and meters. They are your Transmission and Distribution Utility (TDU) and handle delivery and outages, no matter which plan you pick. A retail plan, like the ones Energy Direct helps you choose, sets your price, terms, and billing.

Neighborhoods from The Heights and Westchase to Katy, Pearland, and Sugar Land can see different pricing by ZIP and usage. That is why a smart comparison starts with your address, not a single "Houston average rate."

Compare a Houston plan in 5 steps
  1. Enter your ZIP to see plans in the CenterPoint area.
  2. Open the plan's Electricity Facts Label (EFL).
  3. Compare price at 500 / 1000 / 2000 kWh to match your home.
  4. Watch for minimum-usage fees and bill-credit thresholds.
  5. Confirm contract length and any early-termination fee, then enroll.

What to check on the EFL

Every Texas plan has an Electricity Facts Label. Use it to compare apples to apples:

  • Average price at 500 / 1000 / 2000 kWh — apartments vs. larger homes.
  • Base charges or minimum-usage fees — fees if you use too little.
  • Bill credits and thresholds — at what usage they apply.
  • Contract length & early-termination fee — what leaving early costs.
  • Renewable content — the renewable percentage, if that matters to you.

Typical Houston usage

  • Apartments: ~750–1000 kWh / month
  • Single-family homes: ~1200–2000+ kWh / month
  • Summer: higher, from long A/C run-time and humidity

EFL pricing is shown at 500 / 1000 / 2000 kWh, so knowing your usage makes the right plan obvious.

Houston electricity questions

Will my lights go out when I switch providers?

Typically no. CenterPoint keeps delivering power while your new plan begins billing.

Who is the TDU in Houston?

CenterPoint Energy is the Transmission and Distribution Utility for most of the Houston area. They manage lines, outages, and delivery.

How do I compare rates by ZIP code?

Enter your ZIP on the plans page to see options for your address. Rates and availability vary by ZIP and usage across Houston neighborhoods.

Can I choose a renewable plan in Houston?

Many plans in the CenterPoint area include renewable content. Check the renewable percentage on each plan's EFL.
