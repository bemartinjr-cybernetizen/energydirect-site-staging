Why comparing by ZIP beats a "Houston average"

A single citywide average hides what you would actually pay. Two things drive your real price, and both move by address.

Your delivery charges depend on your utility

Most of Houston sits in the CenterPoint Energy delivery area, but the exact delivery charges that flow onto your bill are tied to your meter, not to a citywide number. A ZIP lookup pulls the plans priced for your address.

  • Same poles and wires: CenterPoint delivers the power no matter which plan you choose.
  • Different plan menus: available terms and offers can vary by neighborhood, from The Heights and Katy to Pearland and Sugar Land.

Your home's usage changes the best plan

A small apartment and a four-bedroom house rarely land on the same plan. The price that looks low at 1,000 kWh can change once minimum-usage fees and bill credits are factored in at your real usage.

  • Match the plan to your usage, not to a headline rate.
  • Check the kWh tiers the plan prices at, so the number you see is the number you live with.

What to read on every Electricity Facts Label

The EFL is the one document that tells you the truth about a plan. Open it for each plan before you enroll.

The numbers that move your bill

  • Price at 500 / 1,000 / 2,000 kWh: matched to your home size.
  • Minimum-usage fees: charges that hit if you use too little.
  • Bill credits: the usage level where any credit kicks in.
  • TDU delivery charges: the CenterPoint line items, listed separately.

The terms that move your options later

  • Rate type: fixed plans hold the energy charge for the term, variable plans can change month to month.
  • Contract length: and what happens at the end of the term.
  • Early-termination fee: what leaving early would cost.
  • Renewable content: the green percentage, if that matters to you.
